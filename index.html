About Ann

No Stranger to extreme degrees, Ann Chafoulias was born in famously freezing Rochester, Mn. She embraced desert living while attending Arizona State where she earned her degree in the Health Sciences.

As a proud Alumni, Ann remains actively involved in ASU’s development initiatives. Ann’s passion in real estate is for her client’s to love where they live and maximize all the valley of the sun has to offer. Let’s Talk

neighborhoods

Central Corridor

Not to be underestimated. For Minneapolis friends, think Uptown and Downtown! Young, fun walkable to trendy secret gems of the city. Local unique businesses and restaurants. No chin-anything here!

Biltmore Corridor

The Chicago Wrigley family was on to something when they built their now famous Winter get away mansion right next to the world Famous Biltmore hotel. Homes here are nestled in this exquisite, classic neighborhood.

A fusion of charm and convenience. Mid westerners feel at home with more greenery, rather than the desert landscaping.

Arcadia

Once known for acres of orange groves, Arcadia has morphed into a special gem of an area. Highly desirable due to its unique blend of greenery and amazing Valley of the Sun location.

You can go to Old Scottsdale one direction and head down-town the other direction! Class meets charm in Arcadia from original ranch style homes to newly remastered showcase homes all enjoying spectacular views of the Camelback mountain.

Paradise Valley

Considered one of the most exclusive zip codes in the nation! Reknown for it's exquisite golf and country club lifestyle. Residents of paradise valley enjoy larger lots and privacy. May areas within PV enjoy amazing Mountain views.

Old Town Scottsdale

There are reasons people flock to old town Scottsdale. Yes, tourists come here to enjoy golf, shopping and fun, but living here is fabulous as well. As a resident of Scottsdale, you will enjoy convenience to everyday living and really feel that you are on vacation every day.

To my Edina Mn friends, Old town Scottsdale is the Southwest version of 50th and France!
